Why Process the Plastic Waste?

The production of plastic is vast and continues to grow, driven by its affordability and versatility. This scale of production results in a correspondingly large amount of waste. Mismanagement of plastic waste exacerbates the problem. In many regions, collection systems are inadequate, leading to plastic littering landscapes, waterways, and oceans. Once in the environment, plastic breaks down into smaller particles called microplastics, which are persistent and can accumulate toxic chemicals. These microplastics enter the food chain, posing potential risks to human health as well. The disposal of plastic waste through landfilling is also problematic. Plastic takes hundreds of years to decompose fully, contributing to the longevity of landfill sites and releasing greenhouse gases like methane as it breaks down. Efforts to mitigate plastic waste involve a combination of reducing single-use plastics, improving waste management infrastructure, promoting recycling, and supporting innovations in biodegradable plastics.

1
Deposit

Users insert empty plastic bottles or containers into the reverse
vending machine. These machines are strategically placed
in public areas such as supermarkets, parks, or recycling centers
to encourage convenient recycling. Upon insertion, the machine
may verify the material type and condition to ensure it meets
recycling criteria.

2
Shredding

Once deposited, the reverse vending machine takes in the plastic
waste by type using sensors and barcode scanners. For the plastic to
reduce its volume, it is run through a shredder, which helps in
compaction,making transportation more efficient by minimizing the
space required and preparing the material for subsequent handling.

3
Transportation

The cleaned and shredded plastic is then ready for transportation
to specialized recycling facilities. Depending on the scale of
operations and logistics, transportation may occur when the
reverse vending machine reaches capacity or at scheduled
intervals.

4
Further Processing

At the recycling facility, the shredded plastic undergoes further
processing. This typically involves melting at high temperatures.
Once melted, the plastic can be extruded into new forms such as
pellets or sheets. These recycled materials serve as raw materials
for manufacturing various plastic products, thereby closing
the recycling loop.
