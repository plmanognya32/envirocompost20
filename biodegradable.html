Why Process the Organic Waste?

The main source of garbage is generated in our houses. Because of the massive volume of garbage being produced, landfills are overflowing and poor waste management is occurring. These waste materials originate from our homes and workplaces and come in a variety of sizes, forms, and compositions. Household garbage that can eventually be broken down by bacteria or fungi is known as bio-waste. This can take anything from two days to nearly two months, or even longer. Poultry waste doesn't break down; instead, it only draws various pests to it, despite the common misconception that it is part of bio-waste. When handled correctly, biowaste may easily be composted in a short amount of time. But because incorrect segregation techniques were used, this kind of waste cannot process itself in the amount of time it uses.

1
Waste Placement

Users place waste on the segregation plate, which is equipped
with sensors for automated analysis. Which analyzes if the percentage
of plastic contents in the waste is more than 60% or less than 60%.

2
Degradability Analysis

Sensors on the segregation plate analyze the quantity of plastic waste
and determine its degradability percentage. This analysis assesses how
easily and naturally the waste can break down or decompose.

3
Segregation Process

According to the analytical results, the segregation plate automatically
divides the trash into two categories:
Organic waste is biodegradable and appropriate for composting.
Non-organic waste cannot be biodegraded and must be treated differently.

4
Waste Crushing

Organic waste identified by the segregation plate proceeds to the
waste crusher. Here, the waste is mechanically crushed into smaller,
more manageable pieces. This process prepares the organic waste
for further decomposition and composting.

5
Vibration Separation

The waste that passes through the crusher now falls onto a
segregation plate. This plate ensures that non-degradable
elements present in the accepted waste is segregated.

6
Composting Preparation

Organic waste that has been crushed and prepared through the initial
stages is transferred to the composting unit. This unit creates a
controlled environment conducive to the natural decomposition process.

7
Composting and Larvae Hosting

Within the composting unit, the waste undergoes composting
facilitated by larvae. Larvae naturally break down organic matter,
accelerating the composting process. This controlled environment
ensures optimal conditions for decomposition, yielding
nutrient-rich compost in just 20 days.

8
Processing the Compost

The compost is obtained after a period of 20 days. This compost procured
from the machine and transported to refining facilities, where
the compost is segregated, refined, and then packed.
